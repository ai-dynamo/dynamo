NVIDIA Dynamo is an open-source inference framework for serving generative AI workloads in distributed environments. It is Kubernetes-native for production, while the same frontend, router, and worker stack also runs in local containers for evaluation or incremental adoption.

## Why Dynamo

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Performance & Runtime" icon="regular gauge-high">
    Disaggregated serving, KV cache-aware routing, and KV cache offloading, composed over the NIXL low-latency transfer layer.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Backends & Ecosystem" icon="regular microchip">
    Serve vLLM, SGLang, and TensorRT-LLM behind one OpenAI-compatible API. Swap engines without changing your serving infrastructure.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Kubernetes-Native" icon="regular cubes">
    Operator, CRDs, autoscaling, multinode scheduling, and an inference gateway — Kubernetes-native, but not Kubernetes-only.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Open & Community-Driven" icon="regular users">
    Open source and developed in the open. Adopt one component at a time, or run the full stack.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Dynamo optimizes the system around the inference engine: it adds the distributed layer — disaggregated serving, smart routing, KV cache management across memory tiers, and autoscaling — on top of any supported backend.
